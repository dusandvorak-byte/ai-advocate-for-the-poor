# OpenAI Build Week — pre-submission checklist

Source of truth: [Official Rules](https://openai.devpost.com/rules). Recheck them immediately before submission because they may be amended.

## Hard eligibility and deadline

- [ ] Entrant has joined the hackathon on Devpost and is eligible in the country of residence.
- [ ] Submit by **July 21, 2026, 5:00 PM Pacific Time** — **July 22, 2026, 02:00 CEST (Prague)**. Do not rely on the converted time without a final timezone check.
- [ ] Choose exactly one track: **Apps for Your Life**.

## Project and evidence of new work

- [ ] The public demo works for free without restrictions and matches the video and description.
- [ ] Clearly identify this as a pre-existing prototype materially extended after July 13, 2026, 09:00 PT.
- [ ] Preserve dated commits and the primary Codex thread as evidence of Build Week work.
- [ ] Run `/feedback` in the primary Codex build thread and copy its Session ID into Devpost.
- [ ] README explains how Codex and GPT-5.6 were used, where Codex accelerated work, and which product, engineering, safety, and design decisions remained human decisions.

## Required submission materials

- [ ] English project description accurately distinguishes current functionality from vision.
- [ ] Public YouTube demo is **under 3 minutes**; target 2:30.
- [ ] Video contains spoken audio and clearly demonstrates both the product and how Codex and GPT-5.6 were used.
- [ ] Video and all testing instructions are in English, or include a complete English translation.
- [ ] Video contains no unlicensed music, third-party trademarks, private documents, addresses, signatures, or personal data.
- [ ] Repository URL and live demo URL are correct.
- [ ] Repository has the MIT license, setup steps, sample data, test command, and deployment instructions.
- [ ] Submit early, then reopen the Devpost entry and verify that every field, link, video, and Session ID is present.

## Rights, privacy, and legal safety

- [ ] Every public case-study source is owned, licensed, public-domain, or published with valid permission.
- [ ] The author's consent covers the named case study; consent does not extend automatically to relatives, witnesses, health data, addresses, signatures, or other third parties.
- [ ] Public officials are quoted accurately, in context, and by reference to the source; allegations are visibly labelled as allegations.
- [ ] No claim says that a referral proves wrongdoing, that an allegation is a finding, or that a procedural decision decided the merits.
- [ ] No third-party document is published merely because it was uploaded during development.

## Final technical gate

- [ ] `npm test` passes.
- [ ] GitHub Pages publishes only `web/`.
- [ ] Unsupported and modified inputs are rejected.
- [ ] Czech and English interfaces expose the same working demonstration.
- [ ] Demo remains available at least through the end of judging.

## Do not submit yet if

- the `/feedback` Session ID is missing;
- the video is not public, has no spoken explanation, or is 3:00 or longer;
- the Build Week additions cannot be distinguished from pre-existing work;
- any public material contains personal data or third-party content without a clear right to publish;
- the live demo does not match what the video claims.
