WHY IT EXISTS

When you know what happened but cannot yet prove it

An official letter arrives on Friday afternoon. It contains reference numbers, links to earlier proceedings, and perhaps a deadline. You do not know whether or when the clock started. The person who could advise you will not be available until next week.

What happened?What does the evidence show?What remains unknown?What must a person check?

This is where AI Advocate for the Poor begins: between “I know what happened” and “I can prove it.” It places a new document in the story, shows what it changes, and says openly what it does not know.

AI Advocate turns an overwhelming archive into auditable memory, so a person does not have to be wealthy, healthy, or legally trained to understand their own story.That memory should belong to the person.
Other moments when a person may lose the whole story
Fire or flood

Damaged records, photographs, and receipts remain. The fire service, police, and insurer each hold only one part.

A death in the family

Memories, letters, and official records are dispersed among relatives around the world.

An accident

Reports, expert opinions, photographs, and testimony accumulate, while some statements change over time.

Poverty here is not only a lack of money. Faced with a complex system, anyone can become poor in time, health, information, professional support, or the ability to keep the whole story connected.

WHO IT IS FOR

Anyone who needs to understand their own documents without losing their story

After a relative dies, a fire, an accident, an insurance letter, or an exchange with a public authority, context is easily lost. The system creates a private personal or family memory and prepares a clear, source-linked overview for family, an advice service, a lawyer, or another person the user trusts.

It is practical infrastructure for democracy and solidarity: it shows who said what, when, from which source, what changed, and what is still missing. The extensive cannabis records are only a demanding controlled test set, not the service’s topic or political position.

It does not guarantee success, create a legal entitlement, or replace a professional. It helps a person retain their own memory and hand it to another human in a reviewable form.

            PROJECT MISSION

            From thousands of fragmented items to an auditable case

            A person with limited money or health may have a legitimate claim and enough evidence, yet still fail because they lack the resources, time, or professional capacity to organize their case.

            AI Advocate is designed to compensate for this disadvantage. It turns fragmented legal and life materials into a structure of people, institutions, proceedings, events, claims, and evidence. It can then gather the relevant sources within a selected branch and prepare a proposed next step for human review.

            fragmented materials→auditable structure→relevant branch→proposed action

            This does not replace a lawyer. It helps a person reach the point where a lawyer, legal-aid organization, ombudsperson, or public authority can actually help.
