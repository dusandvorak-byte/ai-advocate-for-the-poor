# Pre-deadline update — 21 July 2026

After submitting the entry on 20 July 2026, we continued controlled testing before the hackathon deadline. The exact submission website remains preserved in the public dated archive; the live site now shows the separately labelled v3.2 pre-deadline development state.

We applied the same provenance, privacy, citation, and non-transfer rules to 18 additional private files. Deduplication reduced them to 17 distinct byte streams and 15 substantive records across four separate personal and institutional branches. No new private original was published. Exact reviewed inputs are recognized locally by full SHA-256 fingerprint; an altered or foreign document cannot inherit prepared case conclusions.

The clearest evidence of usefulness was restraint. The system did not rewrite conditional discontinuance as a conviction, did not recommend reopening without the decision that would be challenged, exposed differing plant counts as a primary-record retrieval task rather than declaring judicial error, and kept each person’s procedural status separate. It also identified a shared laboratory-review question while preserving it as a creator-confirmed legal hypothesis and requiring case-specific verification.

The interface was redesigned around a simple pattern: **one sentence → traffic light → next human step → expandable evidence**. Four anonymized case rows now show the supplied source inventory, current status, candidate new evidence, and legal review axes without assigning a probability of success. In all four cases, the shared new evidence concerning the absence and lack of unification of a binding THC-measurement procedure receives **9/9 — EXTREME, ON FIRE**. The traffic light measures relevance only; it does not predict a court’s future decision.

The same public tool remains useful outside this controlled legal test. An unrelated insurance notice receives a private 0/9 archive result, preserves only the deadline or sanction explicitly present in the document, and propagates nothing into another person’s case.

The latest five-file intake demonstrates evidence memory rather than mere file counting. Three files concern already-known events: one exact police duplicate increases nothing, while a fifteen-page reopening motion and forty-nine-page preventive filing upgrade two creator-recorded events to primary-PDF-established content. Two files create new official nodes: Ministry of the Interior response **MV-114818-2/TP-2026** and Brno Regional Public Prosecutor review branch **1 KZT 475/2026-32**. The interface shows the state before and after each insertion, the branches it may affect, and the evidentiary boundary. It does not rewrite the ministry response as a merits assessment or the announced prosecutor review as its outcome. No private original was published.

A separate exact record provides direct judicial evidence of the workflow’s value. On 29 July 2025, by order ref. **11 To 88/2024-2990**, the High Court in Prague quashed the findings of guilt, related sentences, and forfeiture order in the Prague Municipal Court judgment of 7 May 2024, ref. **45 T 1/2024-2430**, and returned the matter to the Prague Municipal Court for a new hearing and decision under Section 259(1) of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The order separately criticizes lay chemical reasoning, the failure to distinguish lawful from prohibited cannabis, unclear treatment of a 1.0% THC result, and insufficient judicial review of expert evidence. Its central stated ground remains the judgment’s complete unreviewability. The creator reports that the new hearing is scheduled for October 2026; because that date is not in the order, the interface labels it separately as creator-confirmed.
